# Coffee Brewing Basics

## Espresso

Espresso is a concentrated coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee under high pressure (around 9 bars). A standard double shot uses 18 grams of coffee and yields about 36 grams of liquid in 25 to 30 seconds.

The grind for espresso should be very fine, similar to powdered sugar. If the shot pulls too fast and tastes sour, grind finer. If it pulls too slow and tastes bitter, grind coarser.

Water temperature should sit between 90 and 96 degrees Celsius. Most home machines preheat the group head; commercial machines hold the boiler at a stable temperature.

## Pour Over

A pour over uses a paper filter and a slow, controlled stream of water. Common devices include the V60, Chemex, and Kalita Wave. The ratio is typically 1 gram of coffee to 16 grams of water.

Grind size for pour over is medium-fine, similar to table salt. Brew time should be between 3 and 4 minutes total.

## Beans

Coffee beans come from two main species: Arabica and Robusta. Arabica is sweeter and more aromatic, while Robusta is more bitter and contains roughly twice the caffeine.

Beans are best used within four weeks of roasting. Store in an opaque, airtight container at room temperature. Never freeze ground coffee.
